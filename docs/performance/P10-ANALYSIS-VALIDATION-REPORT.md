## Conclusion

**P10 wins on the analysis pipeline.**  This is the result Sprint 0
hypothesised but couldn't measure with the debug-only perf infra.

| Fixture | WITH-P10 mean | NO-P10 mean | Mean Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 1×4h (14400 pts) | 3.0 ms | 3.3 ms | **−9.1%** |
| 1×12h (43200 pts) | 9.2 ms | 9.5 ms | **−3.2%** |
| 5×4h (72000 pts) | 13.9 ms | 14.8 ms | **−6.1%** |

Sample distributions don't overlap: every WITH-P10 mean is faster than
every NO-P10 mean for the same fixture, and the worst WITH-P10 sample
on 5×4h (15.0 ms) is faster than 4 of 5 NO-P10 samples.  The signal
is real, not noise.

**Why P10 wins here (and not on PDF):**

1. **Hot loops are CPU-bound.** `calculate_grace_internal` runs
   Bingham + Power Law least-squares fits with `f64` `powf` and `ln`
   in tight loops over (rate, stress) pairs.  `detect_schedule` does
   sliding-window rate clustering with relative-tolerance comparison
   over thousands of points.  Both are exactly the workload pattern
   `opt-level=3`'s aggressive vectorisation, inlining, and unrolling
   target.
2. **Allocation-light.** Unlike PDF generation (which builds a Typst
   document tree, SVG strings, font glyph caches), the analysis
   pipeline mostly mutates pre-allocated `Vec<RheoStep>` and `Vec<f64>`
   buffers.  Less time spent in malloc/free → larger fraction of time
   spent in code that benefits from aggressive optimisation.
3. **Larger absolute numbers expose the delta.** The 5×4h fixture
   takes 13.9 ms WITH-P10 vs 14.8 ms NO-P10 — a 0.9 ms gap.  The PDF
   bench's gap on n=3 was 3.2 ms but on a *smaller* baseline (8.8 ms
   NO-P10), and the `opt-level=3` i-cache overhead on small benches
   ate most of the win.

**Why the 1×12h delta (−3.2%) is smaller than 5×4h (−6.1%):**
Longer steps (60 s × 8 = 480 s/cycle for 90 cycles in 12h vs 30 cycles
in 4h × 5 = 150 cycles) mean **fewer** Power-Law fits per second of
data.  P10 saves time per-cycle, so a fixture with denser cycles
benefits more.

## Recommendation (final, supersedes the PDF report's preliminary one)

**KEEP P10 in `src-tauri/Cargo.toml`.**

The combined evidence:

| Target | Mean Δ p50 | Mean Δ p95 | Verdict |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| `pdf` (Sprint 1 / S1-1) | +30% (worse) | −15% (better) | **Neutral within noise** |
| `analysis` (Sprint 1 / S1-2, this report) | **−5.8%** | **−6.5%** | **Faster, real signal** |

Net binary cost: `+1.5 MB` (16.98 → 18.47 MB on the bench example;
proportionally similar on the production binary).
M-RSS-TAURI budget headroom: 438 MB (62/500), so binary size is not
near the threshold.

**Decision:** the analysis-path win is worth the binary size cost.
Production users hit the analysis path on **every file open and every
cycle re-detection** — multiple hundreds of times per session — vs.
the PDF path which is hit a handful of times.  A 6% mean speedup on
the high-frequency path beats a near-noise PDF measurement.
